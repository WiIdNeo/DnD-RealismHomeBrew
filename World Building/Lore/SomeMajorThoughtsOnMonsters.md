# Mechanics

Many Monsters in DnD are Lore-wise really powerful beings and in fight they are just only some higher Damage claws or Bites, especially if those monsters are giant or bigger size that feels often really bad.

For Example if a Taresque is Attacking by claws this foot is like a house big. There is no sigle Target at all. I would handle such attacks as AoE if logic is better than. Also, the strength is immense, even if you call to not want your players to die on being hit by a slap of a house big claw (what may be the logic consequence) at least stance damage modifier needs to be really high to avoid a lazy paladin to block that attack, that's just not logically possible.

Especially the fights against giant monsters become really threatening, due to pure power those giants got. Blocking mostly is not possible, even parrying a monster of giant size or a giant itself is really unrealistic. On a humanoid like a giant I would allow it I guess, as parry only means to control the enemy's hit to another direction and therefore exposing it. So I guess that's possible with all kinds of weapon wielding monster, but if it is just fighting with it's fists or claws that becomes quite impossible, and even harder on high strength or bigger sizes.

Therefor my monsters not only are a stat-block and some generic copy past attacks, but on each monster will be an more detailed mechanic explanation.

# Lore

The real reason I now started this project was me trying to find a devil could fulfill a specific role and just not be able to find kind of this monster. It should just not be evil to the players but help them in cost of them helping the devil later on. But there was no Devil really fitting this role because of the Alignment System. 

Therefore and as consequence of my merge of higher planes into material world, I am resolving the strong alignment, may changing the alignment of monsters to fit their role better. This resolves bounding against lore and creates (volatile) fractions based on current goals and flaws, what makes the world little harder to explain but also more logic. As now a demon could be neutral or also help the demons because of it's own will, I will still write down some generic alike: Demons and Devils hate each other and so very unlikely not killing each other on sight. But there will be an extra paragraph like: But who knows what happens if they share the same enemy as in the war against celestials? Could Demon and Devil lastly fight together for they have the same enemy? So the DM get's an input for shaping their world and the current demons will. May there is a fraction hate the Celestials more than the Demons, so they are helping the demons in fight against celestial. Of course this bounding is volatile due to concrete Enemy, but as long that lives, it may last.
