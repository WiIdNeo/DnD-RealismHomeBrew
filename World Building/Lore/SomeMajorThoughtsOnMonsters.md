# Mechanics

Many Monsters in DnD are Lore-wise really powerful beings and in fight they are just only some higher Damage claws or Bites, especially if those monsters are giant or bigger size that feels often really bad.

For Example if a Taresque is Attacking by claws this foot is like a house big. There is no sigle Target at all. I would handle such attacks as AoE if logic is better than. Also, the strength is immense, even if you call to not want your players to die on being hit by a slap of a house big claw (what may be the logic consequence) at least stance damage modifier needs to be really high to avoid a lazy paladin to block that attack, that's just not logically possible.

Especially the fights against giant monsters become really threatening, due to pure power those giants got. Blocking mostly is not possible, even parrying a monster of giant size or a giant itself is really unrealistic. On a humanoid like a giant I would allow it I guess, as parry only means to control the enemy's hit to another direction and therefore exposing it. So I guess that's possible with all kinds of weapon wielding monster, but if it is just fighting with it's fists or claws that becomes quite impossible, and even harder on high strength or bigger sizes.

Therefor my monsters not only are a stat-block and some generic copy past attacks, but on each monster will be an more detailed mechanic explanation.

### Framework recap (the four tiers)
1. **No faith — agents.** Devils, demons, celestials, yugoloths. Defined by service/contract, not belief. 
2. **No faith — forces of nature others react to.** Giants, ettins, ogres, trolls, oni. Nobody worships them; mortals propitiate them.
3. **Animist believers with a cultural veneer.** Beast-folk — a shared base layer of spirit/ancestor belief (no fixed pantheon), dressed in whichever neighboring culture's ritual language they picked up.
4. **Full organized pantheons.** Your core playable races, dragons, some outliers.

### Fey 

Fairies/Pixies/Sprites, Korreds, Quicklings, Redcaps — Celtic, Hags — Slavic. Drow — worth considering West African Anansi-trickster-spider lineage, inverted into a tyrant matriarch, as a fresher real-world pairing than treating it purely as a corrupted branch of the Celtic elf pantheon

### Giants & "forces of nature"

Giants, Ettins, Trolls, Ogres — Norse jötnar model, no clergy, propitiation not worship. Oni — Japanese, but worth a correction: oni aren't purely Shinto. They're a **Buddhist-hell-and-Chinese-demon (gui) import** that got folklorized into native Japanese belief — closer to "imported hell-iconography gone folk" than animist Shinto. Feared and warded off (bean-throwing at Setsubun), never worshipped — same no-faith model applies.
