I found it a big mistake in design about the Great Wheel, that if travel to upper planes it ends up you get into a perfectly balanced world and you as invader are the only conflict you find. In my opinion a world should just have all it's own conflicts and progressions without anyone looking or any player being there. Therefor the good planes and especially the lawful-good planes are really boring, as all the conflicts as the war against evils takes place mostly outside the upper planes.

To fix that issue I connect all upper planes to one world, That works perfectly, as there are planes just being the point where two other planes meet each other. But as this would just create a second planet little harder than the normal one I would also Merge those! This means you have more like places on earth for each plane, like an olymp or a giant scar in world as the abyss. 

Of course this idea causes Toril or however you call your planet to expand earth size by for sure four times. But actually I don't see any problem with that, as players never saw all of Toril in one campane and maps shift anyway. Also there is magic in world so even if you stick to a physical positioned place for abyss like I do, it can be endless in meaning itself is like a ring under the surface of world, and shifting nature makes it even more endless, even if space itself is limited. Or you take another way of handling, by making the entrances physically exist and also connections and relations under 'planes', but the plane itself is in like another place, so you can extend this truely to infinity. But that's just some phelosophy, as in a Story you don't really care how far you actually traveled. The DM say it was this far and so it was that far, no matter how many meters you really moved.
