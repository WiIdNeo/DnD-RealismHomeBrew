# Weather

> ### Problem:
> Weather in DnD is not really implemented, but a realistic world for sure got weather and temperatures.
> So this is mainly about Weather pheanomia and their impact. You will also find tables to dice weather per biome.

Each Biome got different rain-chance, temperatures and cloud-building. Also wind is harshly influenced by Biome.

Those Grids are provided by claude.ai, as I have no clue about realistic whether phenomena.

*Notes for the DM:*
- *Temperature deviations are relative to whatever seasonal baseline you've set for that biome; the same table works year-round.*
- *Below-freezing results (actual temperature) should be narrated as snow, sleet, or blizzard as appropriate rather than rain — the "Rain" column stays the same, only your description changes.*
- *"Storm"/"Devastating Storm" wind entries can be flavored per biome (sandstorm in deserts, gale at sea, blizzard-force gusts in polar/tundra regions, etc.) without needing separate table entries.*


## Weather effects
### Clouds

Clouding does not really have an impact on gameplay. It is a little darker, or don't get blindeed by sun. But that is mainly world description and gate for rain.

### Rain

Rain of course only appears if there are clouds.

Rain occures in different scales:

|scale|effect|
|-|-|
no rain | no effect
drizzle | world description
rain | ground and cloths gets wet, DEX: -1
heavy rain | ground is muddy now, DEX: -2
thunderstorms | lightnings 


### Wind

Wind also occures in different scales.

|scale|effect|
|-|-|
| no wind | temperature +1
normal wind | no effect
strong wind | temperature -1, balance-check -1
storm | winding objects, balance-checks -2
devastating storm | greater flying objects, balance-checks -3

### Temperatures
Temperature can also vary from day to day. This of course does not mean a table for each temperature, but the difference from normal one, reaching from -5 to 5

Temperature is an indirect debuff applied. 

|Absolute temperature | effect |
|-|-|
<-30°C | ...
<-20°C | start to freeze in normal winter clothes
<-10 | start getting cold in winter clothing 
<0°C | water starts freezing
<5°C | winter clothing needed
<10°C | summer suite is too cold
|>20°C | exhausting in winter clothes
|>30°C | exhausting in Normal clothes
|>40°C | exhausting no matter what clothes
