In DnD your Character just works. There is really low care needed to keep him alive and powerful. But in Real Life that's a little harder, for example you can't just eat all three days without hunger and long-term problems. I try to address this problem without make it a bookkeeping nightmare. 

I try this by giving a per hour or per day need of those things and your DM decides how oft to ask the players about it. I will add a personal recommendation. 

# Needs of a character

- Food
- Water
- Rest
- Warmth, but no heat
- health

## Character Needs – Expanded House Rules

The core principle remains the same as in the original version: **the DM rarely asks for tracking** (usually once per day during a rest), while a simple, realistic deterioration system runs in the background if a need is not met. The common mechanic throughout is **Exhaustion**.

---

## Food

**Consumption:** 1/24 of one day's ration per hour (official item: *Rations (1 day)*).

**Tracking Frequency:** Once per day, typically during a Long Rest. This is sufficient for normal exploration.

In reality, humans can survive roughly **three weeks without food**, but gameplay becomes increasingly difficult after only a few days. Beginning with the first missed day, the Constitution saving throw DC rises until no save can prevent deterioration.

| Days Without Food  | Effect                                                                                              |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 day              | No mechanical effect; roleplay only (hunger, irritability, stomach growling)                        |
| 2 days             | DC 10 Constitution save; failure = gain 1 level of Exhaustion                                       |
| 3 days             | DC 13 (+3 cumulative for each additional day)                                                       |
| 4–5 days           | DC 16–19; additionally, disadvantage on Strength and Dexterity checks regardless of the save result |
| 6–7 days           | DC 22+; realistically very difficult to succeed                                                     |
| 7+ days            | No saving throw; automatically gain 1 Exhaustion level each day                                     |
| Exhaustion Level 6 | Death by starvation                                                                                 |

**Recommendation:** Treat the first missed meal purely as flavor. Players should only begin feeling meaningful consequences once deprivation becomes significant, keeping bookkeeping to a minimum.

---

## Water

**Consumption:** 1/24 of a waterskin per hour (roughly 1 liter/day under normal conditions; more in hot climates or during strenuous activity—see Temperature).

**Tracking Frequency:** Can usually be checked together with food. In deserts, glaciers, or similarly water-scarce environments, consider using the half-day schedule below.

Water is much more critical than food (the "three days without water" rule), so progression occurs in **12-hour intervals** instead of full days.

| Half-Days Without Water (12h) | Effect                                                                                             |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 (12 h)                      | No mechanical effect; simply thirsty                                                               |
| 2 (24 h)                      | DC 10 Constitution save; failure = 1 Exhaustion level                                              |
| 3 (36 h)                      | DC 12; additionally, disadvantage on checks requiring concentration (including maintaining spells) |
| 4 (48 h)                      | DC 14                                                                                              |
| 5 (60 h)                      | DC 16                                                                                              |
| 6 (72 h / 3 days)             | DC 18; last saving throw that can prevent further decline                                          |
| 7+ (84 h+)                    | No saving throw; automatically gain 1 Exhaustion level every additional 12-hour period             |
| Exhaustion Level 6            | Death by dehydration (typically after 3–4 days)                                                    |

### Heat Modifier

While traveling in deserts or under severe heat (see Temperature, beginning at **Exhausting Heat**), **all time intervals in this table are halved.**

## Sleep / Rest

**Required per day:** 8–9 hours, depending on the day's physical demands (heavy labor or combat requires closer to 9 hours).

Normally, this corresponds to completing a Long Rest.

The progression mirrors real sleep deprivation: mild cognitive impairment after 24 hours, microsleep and hallucinations after 48–72 hours, and psychotic symptoms after roughly four days.

| Time Without Sleep       | Effect                                                                                                     |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Up to 24 h awake         | Disadvantage on Perception and concentration-based checks (roughly equivalent to being mildly intoxicated) |
| 24–48 h (1 missed sleep) | DC 10 Constitution save; failure = 1 Exhaustion level                                                      |
| 48–72 h (2 days)         | DC 13; DM may introduce involuntary microsleep (blackouts lasting 1d6 rounds during calm moments)          |
| 72–96 h (3 days)         | DC 16; mild hallucinations possible (brief harmless illusions)                                             |
| 96–120 h (4 days)        | DC 19; hallucinations may become mechanically relevant and nearly indistinguishable from real illusions    |
| 120 h+ (5+ days)         | No saving throw; automatically gain 1 Exhaustion level each additional day                                 |
| Exhaustion Level 6       | Collapse and death from total exhaustion                                                                   |

**Important:** Simply lying down or dozing without real sleep (for example while standing watch during a storm) counts as only **half-rest**—the clock continues running, just more slowly.

## Temperature

Each character has a **Comfort Temperature** (their personal baseline where no effects occur). The table measures deviation from that value.

Wet clothing worsens cold exposure by one category, while warm clothing or insulated armor improves it by one category.

| Difference from Comfort Temperature | Effect                                                                                                            |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| −30°C or more                       | Death from hypothermia                                                                                            |
| −20°C to −29°C                      | 1d6 cold damage every 10 minutes if unprotected; additionally begin at Exhaustion Level 2                         |
| −10°C to −19°C                      | Speed reduced by 10 ft.; DC 10 Constitution save every hour or gain 1 Exhaustion level                            |
| −1°C to −9°C                        | Uncomfortable but no mechanical effect (flavor: shivering)                                                        |
| 0°C                                 | Neutral; no effect                                                                                                |
| +1°C to +9°C                        | Uncomfortable but no mechanical effect (flavor: sweating)                                                         |
| +10°C to +19°C                      | Gain Exhaustion Level 1 after 1 hour without shade or rest; water consumption doubled                             |
| +20°C to +29°C                      | 1d6 heat damage every 10 minutes in direct sunlight; DC 10 Constitution save each hour or gain 1 Exhaustion level |
| +30°C or more                       | Death from heatstroke                                                                                             |

**Reality Reference:** Without any protection, truly extreme temperatures become life-threatening after roughly **three hours**. This is represented by the rapid escalation from initial Exhaustion after one hour to direct damage after approximately two to three hours.

---

## Health / Disease

Characters who are repeatedly exposed to wet conditions, severe cold, overheating, or poison risk developing fever. Fever represents a gradual decline that **cannot be cured through ordinary rest**. Recovery requires time, treatment, or magical healing.

| Fever Stage          | Example Cause                                                | Effect                                                                                                             |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Elevated Temperature | One exposure to cold while wearing wet clothing              | Disadvantage on Constitution saving throws                                                                         |
| Fever                | Two consecutive exposures or untreated poison                | Additionally reduce movement (as if Slowed)                                                                        |
| High Fever           | Three consecutive exposures or untreated poison for 24 hours | Additionally disadvantage on attack rolls and reduced stamina/endurance                                            |
| Critical Fever       | Four or more exposures or severe poisoning                   | Functions like Exhaustion Level 4 (maximum HP halved); DC 15 Constitution save each day or advance one Fever Stage |
| Terminal             | Untreated for several days                                   | Death unless treated within 24 hours (healing magic, antidote, or proper medical care with bed rest)               |

### Recovery

A Fever Stage can only be reduced by one of the following:

* One full day of bed rest **with adequate food, water, and warmth**
* A healing potion or healing spell of **2nd level or higher**
* A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Medicine) check by a caregiver once per day
